# Case Study 001: CEO Digital Exposure Investigation

## Overview

This case study simulates a privacy assessment conducted for a technology startup CEO preparing for a major funding round.

The objective is to identify publicly available information that could expose the executive and company to privacy, security, reputational, or social engineering risks.

The assessment follows a structured approach:

1. Asset Identification
2. Digital Footprint Mapping
3. Threat Modeling
4. Risk Assessment
5. Privacy Recommendations

## Scope

The investigation focuses on publicly accessible information associated with the CEO, including:

- Professional profiles
- Social media accounts
- Company biographies
- Conference appearances
- Podcast interviews
- Public photographs
- Travel-related disclosures

## Objectives

- Identify exposed personal information
- Determine potential attack vectors
- Evaluate privacy risks
- Assess social engineering opportunities
- Recommend mitigation measures

## Skills Demonstrated

- Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)
- Digital Footprint Analysis
- Threat Modeling
- Privacy Risk Assessment
- Report Writing

## Deliverables

- Executive Summary
- Digital Footprint Map
- Risk Assessment
- Privacy Recommendations
- Risk Score
